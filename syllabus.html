Course Syllabus

PH31207 — Classical Mechanics

Department of Physics, Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur

Course Description

This course introduces the fundamental principles of classical mechanics, beginning with Newtonian mechanics and extending to the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formulations. Emphasis is placed on mathematical methods, physical intuition, and applications to real mechanical systems.

Prerequisites

  • Calculus
  • Vector Algebra
  • Differential Equations
  • Introductory Mechanics

Learning Outcomes

  • Apply Newton's laws to analyze mechanical systems.
  • Understand the principles of oscillatory motion.
  • Use generalized coordinates in mechanical analysis.
  • Formulate and apply Lagrange's equations of motion.
  • Derive and interpret Hamilton's equations.
  • Solve problems involving central force motion.
  • Analyze the dynamics of rigid body motion.

Course Topics

Week Topic
1 Review of Newtonian Mechanics
2 Oscillations
3 Coupled Oscillations
4 Calculus of Variations
5 Lagrangian Mechanics
6 Applications of Lagrange's Equations
7 Central Force Motion
8 Rigid Body Dynamics
9 Hamiltonian Mechanics
10 Canonical Transformations
11 Small Oscillations
12 Revision and Problem Solving
